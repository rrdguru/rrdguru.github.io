Teaching Philosophy

My approach to teaching begins with a simple belief: Before solving a problem, one must first understand it properly.

In many classrooms, the focus quickly shifts toward arriving at the correct answer. My classes instead slow down the process and emphasize structured thinking. Students are encouraged to question assumptions, analyze the structure of problems, and explore alternative explanations before proposing solutions.

Across courses such as Marketing Management, Product Management, Brand Management, and Business Research Methods, students are trained to approach managerial problems as intellectual puzzles. Through case discussions, analytical frameworks, and applied projects, the objective is not simply to transfer knowledge but to develop independent thinking.

This philosophy has evolved through teaching diverse cohorts in Europe, Russia, and India. Across these contexts one lesson has remained constant: when students learn how to think clearly about problems, they gain confidence that extends far beyond the classroom.
