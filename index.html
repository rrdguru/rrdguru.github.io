Who I Am

The Path Here

Most marketing academics come through a single door. I came through three. I started as an electrical engineer, worked full-time at SAIL and Infosys, gained research and industry exposure during my master's at EPFL where I also worked alongside my studies and presented my thesis at a conference, then moved to consulting at Fact Works GmbH in Berlin, and then committed fully to a PhD at the University of Geneva. That sequence was not accidental. Each step added something the previous one could not. The industry grounding shapes the research questions. The research rigour shapes the teaching. The teaching keeps both honest.

Research

What I Study

Quality is not one thing. That is the starting point. When a consumer evaluates a product, they are simultaneously weighing multiple distinct dimensions: functional performance, sensory experience, reliability, symbolic meaning. My doctoral work built a framework and a validated measurement instrument for capturing this multidimensionality. The result was the Customers' Experienced Product Quality (CEPQ) scale, published in the European Journal of Marketing. Since then the questions have deepened. How do brand attachment and quality perceptions together drive loyalty over time? How does this play out across different categories and contexts? And most recently: does the same multidimensional logic apply when the service being evaluated is a university education? These questions now occupy most of my research time, supported by a growing pipeline of longitudinal and collaborative work.

The Growth Arc: Four Phases

1
Phase 1 · 2018 to 2022
Foundation: Geneva and HSE Moscow
  • Teaching Assistant at GSEM. Scored on a 1 to 4 scale, not 5. Average 3.58/4.00 = 89.5% of maximum.
  • Availability rated at or near 4.00/4.00 in every evaluation. The defining strength from day one.
  • DDDM scores improved from 3.43 to 3.61 between 2018 and 2020: the clearest early evidence of a feedback-to-growth loop.
  • HSE Moscow: R and Python taught to Russian undergraduates entirely online. Novelty scores 4.22/5. Technical skills meet cross-cultural delivery.
Scale note: Branding Spring 2018 scored 3.88/4.00 = 97% of maximum. Availability was perfect 4.00/4.00. These are genuinely strong evaluations for a first-time TA in a multilingual, international school.
2
Phase 2 · 2022
First Years at IIM Bodhgaya: Early Peaks and First Friction
  • POMM IPM Jan 2022: course score 4.74, preparation 4.87. Immediate credibility with undergraduate cohorts.
  • Rural Marketing Mar 2022: instructor score 4.76. The life-skill framing lands hardest in this course.
  • PGP MBA courses: a proxy attendance incident in a large cohort (N=173) creates a mid-semester climate shift that the data picks up clearly.
Finding: Undergraduate students respond more strongly to inspiration-led pedagogy. PGP MBA students bring stronger prior expectations and need a different classroom contract. This distinction shapes everything that follows.
3
Phase 3 · 2023
Consolidation and Crisis: The Most Diagnostic Year
  • Record highs in repeat courses: POMM IPM reaches 4.97 preparation, Rural Marketing stays strong.
  • BRM IPM Mar 2023: lowest scores in the dataset. Instructor 2.94, respect score 2.50. A single classroom incident created lasting anxiety for the rest of the semester.
  • Recovery visible by Sep-Oct 2023: Brand Management 4.13, MBA Foundation Marketing 4.68. The pedagogical pivot is working.
Critical pattern: Preparation never drops below 3.95 even at the dataset's lowest point. The crisis was entirely a classroom relationship issue, not a knowledge deficit and not a lack of effort. That distinction matters when reading this data honestly.
4
Phase 4 · 2025
Resolution: IIM Ranchi Peak
  • Preparation 5.00/5.00, unanimous across all 29 respondents. Zero variance. A first in the entire dataset.
  • Instructor vs peers 4.86/5.00. Exam feedback: 25/29 enjoyed it. 0 said useless.
  • Students from engineering and finance backgrounds report permanent shifts in how they see marketing.
The formula, confirmed: Maximum intellectual rigour plus explicitly protected psychological safety plus consistent interactive tools equals the highest scores in seven years of data. This is the floor going forward, not the ceiling.

What Seven Years of Data Says

I teach best when two things are true at the same time: the subject allows for real-world, life-skill framing, and the classroom is a place where being wrong is safe. When both are present, as they were at IIM Ranchi in 2025, students remember specific examples months later, change career intentions and ask to stay in touch. The data is honest about the years when that second condition was not met. The improvement arc is visible. The formula is now known. The task is to make it structural everywhere.

"The very existence of this evaluation dataset, the self-scrutiny, and the observable improvement arc from 2023 to 2025 demonstrate exactly the growth mindset modelled for students."
