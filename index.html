Research

What I Study

The core question is deceptively simple: what does quality actually mean to a consumer? Not as a single score, but as a set of distinct, simultaneously experienced dimensions.
My doctoral work built and validated a measurement instrument for this, the CEPQ scale, published in the European Journal of Marketing. Since then the questions have deepened and expanded. How do quality perceptions, satisfaction and brand attachment drive loyalty differently over time? Do these dynamics hold when the product being evaluated is a management education or a tourism destination?

Teaching

Seven Years of Evidence

How I think about teaching
  • I ask students to understand the problem before they reach for a solution. Most courses jump to frameworks. I make people sit with the problem first, interrogate it, articulate what is actually being asked. The framework comes after.
  • Critical thinking is not something I mention in a syllabus and move on. It means slowing down in class, questioning the first answer that comes up, refusing to let comfortable explanations sit unchallenged.
  • I teach marketing and research as skills, not subjects. A student who has genuinely understood how quality perceptions form will never see a product advertisement the same way again.
  • My industry years are not background colour. Having managed electrical infrastructure in a steel plant and written software, I bring those contexts into class not as anecdotes but as the actual analytical method.
  • The classroom has to be safe enough for genuine thinking to happen. Rigour and safety are not competing values. The best learning happens when both are true at the same time.

The Growth Arc: Four Phases

1
2018 to 2022 · Geneva and HSE Moscow
Foundation
  • Teaching assistant at Geneva GSEM. Scores on a 1-4 scale; average 3.58/4.00 = 89.5% of maximum.
  • Availability rated at or near 4.00/4.00 in every evaluation. The defining strength from day one.
  • HSE Moscow: R and Python taught to Russian undergraduates entirely online. Novelty scores 4.22/5.
2
2022 · IIM Bodhgaya, first year
Early peaks and first friction
  • Principles of Marketing Management (IPM, Jan 2022): course 4.74, preparation 4.87. Immediate traction with undergraduate cohorts.
  • Rural Marketing (PGP, Mar 2022): instructor 4.76. Life-skill framing lands hardest in contextual, case-rich courses.
  • PGP MBA courses: a proxy attendance incident in a large cohort creates a mid-semester climate shift that the data captures clearly.
3
2023 · IIM Bodhgaya, consolidation and crisis
The most diagnostic year
  • Record highs in repeat courses: POMM IPM reaches 4.97 preparation.
  • Business Research Methods (IPM, Mar 2023): lowest scores in the dataset. Instructor 2.94. A single classroom incident created lasting anxiety for the rest of the semester.
  • Recovery visible within the same year: Brand Management 4.13, Foundation Marketing MBA 4.68.
Critical pattern: preparation never drops below 3.95 even at the dataset's lowest point. The crisis was a classroom relationship issue, not a knowledge deficit.
4
2025 · IIM Ranchi
Resolution and peak
  • Preparation 5.00/5.00, unanimous across all 29 respondents. Zero variance. A first in the entire dataset.
  • Instructor vs. peers: 4.86 vs. 4.72. Exam feedback: 25/29 enjoyed it, 0 said useless.
  • Students from engineering and finance backgrounds report permanent shifts in how they see marketing.
Confirmed formula: maximum intellectual rigour plus explicitly protected psychological safety plus consistent interactive tools equals the highest scores in seven years.

Thesis Supervision

These are MSc and BSc dissertations supervised at the University of Geneva between 2015 and 2021. In the European credit system, an MSc thesis represents a full semester of independent research (30 ECTS credits). It is a substantial original piece of work that the student owns completely.

Students came from Management, Business Analytics and Economics backgrounds. Many worked on problems directly connected to the CEPQ quality research programme, making supervision a genuinely collaborative process. Twelve theses completed across seven years.
