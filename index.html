Research

What I Study

The core question is deceptively simple: what does quality actually mean to a consumer? Not as a single score, but as a set of distinct, simultaneously experienced dimensions.

My doctoral work built and validated a measurement instrument for this, the CEPQ scale, published in the European Journal of Marketing. Since then the questions have deepened and expanded. How do quality perceptions, satisfaction, and brand attachment drive loyalty differently over time? Do these dynamics hold when the product being evaluated is a management education or a tourism destination?

Teaching

Academic Leadership & Instruction

Teaching Philosophy

I teach marketing and research as fundamental analytical skills rather than isolated subjects. My approach leverages my background in iron and steel plant operations, software engineering, and strategic consulting. That context sits at the center of how I structure a classroom.

  • Problem First, Framework Second: I require students to interrogate a problem and articulate what is actually being asked before they reach for a solution.
  • Critical Thinking as a Practice: Slowing down in class, questioning the first answer offered, refusing to let comfortable explanations sit unchallenged.
  • Uncompromising Rigour: The best learning occurs when maximum intellectual rigor is paired with explicitly protected psychological safety.
