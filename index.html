The Uncommon Path to Academia

Most marketing scholars come through a single door. My identity is heavily informed by three distinct phases, each shaping how I conduct research and manage a classroom today.

Foundational Leadership at SAU

Currently serving as founding faculty within the newly established Faculty of Management at South Asian University. Establishing an academic department from inception requires immense adaptability. 1. The Industry Root

Five years in software engineering and heavy industrial management across SAIL, Infosys, and Fact Works GmbH Berlin. This grounds my academic work in operational reality.

2. The Methodological Rigor

Doctoral research at the University of Geneva focusing on complex multidimensional scale development. Taught to rely on data, not intuition.

3. The Global Classroom

Teaching across Switzerland, Russia, Mexico, and India. Adapting complex quantitative concepts for highly diverse, cross-cultural student cohorts.

Research Architecture

Quality is not a singular concept. My research investigates how perceived product quality dictates long-term consumer-brand relationships, and how that measurement scales to services like Higher Education.

Current Paradigms

  • Multidimensional Quality Models: Developing scales to capture exact consumer experiences (CEPQ).
  • Brand Loyalty Trajectories: Tracking relationship evolution over time.
  • Sustainable EV Adoption: Live, policy-relevant longitudinal tracking of Indian consumers (in collaboration with UC Davis & Univ. of Geneva).
  • Educational Quality: Analyzing the tension between scaling operations and maintaining excellence in management education.
