Global Insights. Institutional Impact.

My identity as a marketing academic is heavily informed by my roots in the corporate sector. Before entering academia, I spent several years working in software engineering and heavy industrial management across top-tier firms in India and Europe.

Today, my academic career is defined by a borderless perspective. I earned my doctorate in Switzerland and have since instructed diverse student cohorts across Latin America, Eastern Europe, and the Indian subcontinent. This extensive international exposure allows me to bring a distinctly global, empirically grounded framework to both my research initiatives and my classroom pedagogy.

Ecosystem Building & Career Development

Research Architecture

At its core, my research investigates how perceived product quality dictates long-term consumer-brand relationships. I employ multidimensional modeling to capture exactly how consumers experience quality. Recently, I have pushed this framework into new territory, adapting it to evaluate the ultimate service offering: Higher Education.
