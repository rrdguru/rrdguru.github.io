Who I Am

The Path Here

Most marketing academics come through a single door. I came through three. I started as an engineer in heavy industry — SAIL, Infosys, Fact Works GmbH Berlin — then crossed into academia with a doctoral degree from the University of Geneva (GSEM). That transition wasn't a career pivot; it was a deliberate attempt to bring real industrial logic into business education. The research I do and the classes I teach are shaped by that perspective.

Research

What I Study

My research starts with a deceptively simple question: why do people stay loyal to brands they trust, and what breaks that trust? The answer turns out to be multidimensional — it involves how consumers experience quality across different product attributes, how those perceptions compound over time, and how institutions — including universities — can be understood through the same lens. I use structural equation modelling, longitudinal survey designs, and multi-market datasets to find precise answers.

The Growth Arc: Four Phases

1
Phase 1 · 2018–2022
Foundation — Geneva TA & HSE Digital Professor
  • Teaching Assistant at University of Geneva GSEM: Branding, Data Driven Decision Making, Research Methods.
  • Availability rated at or near 4.00/4.00 in every evaluation — a defining strength from day one.
  • Visiting digital professor at HSE Moscow: taught R & Python programming to Russian undergraduates during COVID disruption.
Key data point: DDDM scores improved 3.43 → 3.61 between 2018 and 2020 — the dataset's first evidence of responsive professional growth after receiving critical feedback.
2
Phase 2 · 2022
First Years at IIM Bodh Gaya — Early Peaks
  • POMM IPM (Jan 2022): 4.74 course score, 4.87 preparation — immediate credibility with undergraduate cohorts.
  • Rural Marketing (Mar 2022): 4.76 instructor score — the "marketing as life skill" philosophy resonates powerfully.
  • First PGP friction: a proxy attendance incident (N=173) triggers a climate shift mid-semester — the data's most diagnostic early signal.
Finding: Undergraduate students respond more strongly to inspiration-led pedagogy; postgraduate cohorts require a different classroom contract — a distinction that shapes everything that follows.
3
Phase 3 · 2023
Consolidation & Crisis — The Most Diagnostic Year
  • Record highs in repeat courses: POMM IPM reaches 4.97 preparation score; Rural Marketing remains strong.
  • BRM IPM (Mar 2023): lowest score in dataset — 2.94 instructor, 2.50 respect score — a single classroom incident reverberates.
  • Recovery visible by Sep–Oct 2023: Brand Management (4.13) and MBA Foundation Marketing (4.68) confirm the pedagogical pivot is working.
Critical pattern: Preparation never drops below 3.95 even at the dataset's lowest point. The crisis was entirely a classroom relationship issue — not a knowledge or effort deficit. That distinction matters.
4
Phase 4 · 2025
Resolution & Mastery — IIM Ranchi Peak
  • Preparation: 5.00/5.00 — unanimous across all 29 respondents. Zero variance. A first in the entire dataset.
  • Instructor vs. peers: 4.86/5.00. Exam: 25/29 "Enjoyed it!!", 0 "Useless."
  • Students from engineering and finance backgrounds report permanent shifts in how they see marketing.
The formula, confirmed: Maximum intellectual rigor + explicitly protected psychological safety + consistent interactive tools (Wooclap) = the highest scores in seven years of data.
