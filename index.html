About Me

I'm a marketing scholar with a unique journey from industry to academia. After working in engineering and IT sectors, I pursued my passion for understanding consumer behavior and product quality, earning my PhD from the University of Geneva.

My research explores how product quality shapes consumer-brand relationships and sustainable consumption patterns. I'm particularly interested in how quality perceptions can be leveraged to encourage more sustainable consumer choices.

In the classroom, I emphasize critical thinking and practical applications, helping students see marketing not just as a business function but as a skillset for becoming smarter, more sustainable consumers themselves.

Currently, I'm collaborating with researchers across continents on projects ranging from electric vehicle adoption to tourism destination marketing and healthcare service quality.

Research

Teaching Philosophy & Courses

My teaching approach bridges theory with practice, drawing on my industry experience to make marketing concepts tangible and applicable. I design courses that promote critical thinking through case studies, simulations, and real-world projects.

I believe marketing education should empower students not just as future business leaders but as informed consumers who can make sustainable choices. My courses consistently receive strong evaluations (4.35+/5) for their practical relevance and engaging delivery.
